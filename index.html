Onset Map

Descriptive Overview

1. Tonal Characteristics

  • Attack: Sharper and slightly louder than baseline, producing a clear and immediate onset.
  • Tail: A longer metallic resonance that decays smoothly and evenly.
  • Shimmer: A brief, smooth, higher-pitched transient appearing immediately after the attack.

2. Spatial Behavior

  • Forward Motion: A shallow, linear, centered arc with constant speed.
  • Distance: Short, ending precisely at the bowl’s surface.
  • Surface Contact: Soft and yielding, with no rebound or scattering.

3. Atmospheric Conditions

  • Temperature: Cool and neutral.
  • Density: Thicker than normal, giving a subtle sense of weight.
  • Movement: Faint internal motion with no directional bias.
  • Texture: Coarser granular pockets that shape the dissolution environment.

4. Transition and Cadence

  • Dissolution: The shimmer dissolves into the surrounding air layer.
  • Cadence: Quickened and evenly spaced after the shimmer fades.
  • Return to Normal: Abrupt reset to baseline state.
